All your subscription data in Recur is stored locally on your device using MMKV, a high-performance key-value storage system.

## What Gets Stored

Recur stores the following information locally on your device:

### User Preferences
- **Your name**: The personalized name you enter during onboarding
- **Currency preference**: Your default currency (set once during setup)
- **Theme selection**: Light or dark mode preference
- **Onboarding status**: Whether you've completed the initial setup

### Subscription Data
- **Subscription details**: Name, amount, billing frequency, start date
- **Icons**: Emoji or initials for each subscription
- **Lists**: Custom organization groups (e.g., "Work", "Personal")
- **Order**: Your custom drag-and-drop arrangement of subscriptions

<Info>
  All data is stored in a local database on your device. Nothing is ever sent to external servers or the cloud.
</Info>

## How Data is Stored

Recur uses **MMKV** (Memory Mapped Key-Value), a fast and efficient mobile storage solution:

- **Key-value pairs**: Data is stored as JSON objects with unique keys
- **Validated storage**: All data is validated using Zod schemas before saving
- **Persistent**: Data remains on your device until you delete the app
- **Efficient**: MMKV provides better performance than AsyncStorage or SQLite for this use case

### Storage Keys

While you don't need to know these implementation details, here's what happens behind the scenes:

- `user_name`: Your personalized name
- `onboarding_complete`: Setup completion status
- `subscriptions`: Array of all your subscriptions
- `lists`: Your custom organization lists
- `currency`: Your default currency setting

## Data Persistence

### Data Stays Until Deletion

Your subscription data persists across app sessions and device restarts. As long as Recur is installed on your device, your data remains intact.

### What Happens When You Delete the App

<Warning>
  **Deleting the app permanently erases all your data.** There is no way to recover subscription information after uninstalling Recur.
</Warning>

When you uninstall Recur:

1. **All local data is deleted**: iOS and Android automatically remove app data when you uninstall
2. **No recovery possible**: Since there's no cloud backup, your subscriptions cannot be restored
3. **Complete removal**: No traces of your data remain on the device

This is an intentional privacy feature—your data is never stored anywhere else.

## No Backups or Export

Recur currently does not include:

- **Cloud backups**: No iCloud, Google Drive, or Dropbox integration
- **Export functionality**: No CSV, JSON, or other data export options
- **Sync capabilities**: No cross-device synchronization

<Note>
  If you need to preserve your subscription data before uninstalling, consider taking screenshots of your subscriptions or manually noting them elsewhere.
</Note>

### Why No Backups?

The decision to exclude backups and export features aligns with Recur's privacy-first design:

- **Simpler codebase**: No complex sync logic or cloud integrations
- **Better privacy**: No risk of data leaking through backup services
- **True local-first**: Consistent with the offline-only architecture

## Data Integrity

### Validation and Error Handling

Recur uses runtime validation to ensure data integrity:

- **Zod schemas**: All stored data is validated against TypeScript schemas
- **Error recovery**: Invalid data is logged and filtered out rather than crashing the app
- **Normalization**: When reading subscriptions, malformed entries are automatically cleaned

If you encounter data issues:

1. The app logs warnings but continues functioning
2. Invalid subscriptions are dropped to prevent crashes
3. Valid data is preserved and re-saved

### Updating Subscriptions

When you edit or delete subscriptions:

- **Atomic updates**: Changes are validated before being saved
- **Order preservation**: Custom drag-and-drop order is maintained
- **List relationships**: Deleting a list sets subscriptions to "Unassigned" rather than deleting them

## Storage Limits

MMKV has no practical storage limits for typical use:

- **Scalable**: Can handle hundreds or thousands of subscriptions
- **Fast lookups**: Data retrieval is optimized for quick access
- **Low overhead**: Minimal impact on app size or performance

You're unlikely to hit any storage constraints in normal usage.

## Privacy Benefits

Local-only storage provides several privacy advantages:

### No Data Transmission
- Your subscription amounts, billing cycles, and payment details never leave your device
- No network requests means no risk of data interception
- No servers means no potential for data breaches

### Device Security
- Data is protected by your device's built-in security (biometrics, passcode)
- On iOS and Android, app data is sandboxed and isolated from other apps
- MMKV stores data in the app's private directory

### Complete Deletion
- Uninstalling the app removes all traces of your data
- No residual data on cloud servers
- No data retention policies or backup windows to worry about

<Info>
  Because Recur uses local storage only, your subscription data benefits from your device's existing security features like encryption, biometric locks, and secure boot.
</Info>

## Comparison to Cloud-Based Apps

| Feature | Recur (Local) | Cloud-Based Apps |
|---------|---------------|------------------|
| **Data location** | On your device only | Stored on remote servers |
| **Requires account** | No | Yes |
| **Works offline** | Always | Needs internet for sync |
| **Cross-device sync** | Not available | Available |
| **Data recovery** | Not possible | Backup & restore |
| **Privacy** | Complete | Depends on provider |
| **Export options** | None | Usually available |

Recur's local-first approach trades convenience features like sync and backups for stronger privacy guarantees and complete data ownership.
